Shree Ananthapadmanabha Swamy Lake Temple

A Cultural and Spiritual Gem of Kasaragod

Why is this temple a significant cultural and spiritual destination?

Shree Ananthapadmanabha Swamy Lake Temple, located in Ananthapura, Kasaragod, is the only lake temple in Kerala. Built in the 9th century, it is regarded as the original seat (Moolasthanam) of Lord Ananthapadmanabha.

What makes the temple’s architecture and setting appealing?

The temple is situated at the center of a two-acre lake, fed by a natural spring that remains full year-round. Its traditional Kerala-style architecture features intricate wood carvings of the Dashavatara (ten incarnations of Lord Vishnu).

How does the resident crocodile enhance the temple’s significance?

The temple is home to Babiya, a vegetarian crocodile that feeds on temple prasad. This rare phenomenon enhances the temple’s mystique and spiritual aura.
