Shree Ananthapadmanabha Swamy Lake Temple

A Cultural and Spiritual Gem of Kasaragod

Why is this temple a significant cultural and spiritual destination?

Shree Ananthapadmanabha Swamy Lake Temple, located in Ananthapura, Kasaragod, is the only lake temple in Kerala. Built in the 9th century, it is regarded as the original seat (Moolasthanam) of Lord Ananthapadmanabha, attracting devotees and tourists seeking spiritual and cultural experiences.

What makes the temple’s architecture and setting appealing to tourists?

The temple is situated at the center of a two-acre lake, fed by a natural spring that remains full year-round. Its traditional Kerala-style architecture, featuring intricate wood carvings of the Dashavatara (ten incarnations of Lord Vishnu), enhances its cultural and historical value.

How does the resident crocodile enhance the temple’s significance?

The resident crocodile, Babiya, enhances the temple’s significance by adding a sense of mystique and divine connection. Babiya is believed to be a vegetarian crocodile that survives solely on the temple’s prasad (offerings) of rice and jaggery. This unusual behavior is seen as a divine blessing, attracting spiritual seekers and curious tourists alike. The presence of Babiya symbolizes protection and harmony, reinforcing the temple’s spiritual aura and cultural uniqueness.
